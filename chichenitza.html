Chichen Itza was a vast, sacred city on Mexico's Yucatan Peninsula that was created during the Mayan civilization. It is currently being used as an archaeological site. It was and is a sacred city and Mayan pilgrimage destination created by the Itza, the so-called water sorcerers, in the 5th century AD. Around 3,000 years ago, the Mayans appeared in Guatemala, Honduras, Belize, and Mexico. From 250 until 900 AD, the empire flourished until it collapsed in the south for unexplained reasons. They were known to be devout, therefore Chichen Itza became a symbolic city for them, which it retained beyond its survival.

The most remarkable Mayan Ruin in this site is the Chichen Itza Pyramid, or known as El Castillo. There are also other ruins such as The Ball Game, the Tzompantli, The platform of the eagles and jaguars, The venus platform, Temple of the Warriors, The Thousand Columns Group, and many more.
