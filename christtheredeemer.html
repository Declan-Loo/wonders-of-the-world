Christ the Redeemer is the largest and most famous Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ that stands atop Mount Corcovado in Rio de Janeiro. It is also one of Brazilâ€™s most famous landmarks, as it is affiliated with the city of Rio de Janeiro and the entire country of Brazil. Construction of the statue was finished in 1931. It is 98 feet (30 metres) tall and its arms cover a length of 92 feet (28 metres). It's composed of reinforced concrete with a mosaic of thousands of triangular soapstone tiles, and it stands around 26 feet (8 metres) tall on a square stone pedestal base. 

Pedro Maria Boss, a Vincentian priest, proposed erecting a Christian monument on Mount Corcovado to honour Isabel, Princess Regent of Brazil, in the 1850s. The Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Rio de Janeiro requested in 1921 that a statue of Christ be erected on the peak, which stands at 2,310 feet (704 metres). The statue was first built in France by French sculptor Paul Landowski, who created it with clay. It is then sent to Brazil, where the statue is built with reinforced concrete by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa and French engineer Albert Caquot.
