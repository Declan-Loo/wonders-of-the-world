Christ the Redeemer, Brazil

The symbolic statue of Jesus Christ, situated at the summit of Mount Corcovado in the city of Rio De Janeiro.

Great Wall of China, China

A series of ancient walls and fortifications with a total length of over 13,000 miles. It was erected to fortify against nomadic invaders from the north in the third century BC.

Chichen Itza, Mexico

An archaeological site that was once home to a large city built during the Mayan civilization, located in Yucatan State, Mexico.

Petra, Jordan

Carved directly into the sandstone cliff sides, the ancient city of Petra was once the capital of the Nabataean empire between 400BC and 106AD. It was widely known to be “lost” because it was oblivious to the western world for centuries.

Machu Picchu, Peru

Probably known to be the most amazing urban creation of the Inca Empire at its height. Machu Picchu stands 2,430 m above sea level in the middle of a tropical mountain forest.

Taj Mahal, India

Commissioned in 1632 by Shah Jahan to house the remains of his beloved wife. The mausoleum complex is one of the most outstanding examples of Mughal architecture.

The Colosseum, Italy

An amphitheatre that was built in the year 72 CE, by Roman emperor Vespasian, it was built to entertain the public with gladiator battles, animal fights and et cetera. It is the largest amphitheatre in the Roman history.
